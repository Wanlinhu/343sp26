# From Scratch Roulette A — Reflection

For Roulette A I collaborated with Sasha Hoven — I did audio in Strudel, she did visuals. It was my first time live coding with someone else and the first time using code as a way to collaborate with another person in real time.

I came in without enough practice and felt it. Strudel was still unfamiliar enough that I was spending too much mental energy on syntax rather than actually making musical decisions. We were both figuring things out as we went — neither of us had a clear plan — which made the back and forth interesting even if the output wasn't where I wanted it to be.

My audio ended up going in an EDM direction. The energy was there but the tempo was too fast, and I kept trying to layer things before the previous layer had settled. There was also a feeling of losing control of the code — I was trying my best but the gap between what I heard in my head and what I could actually execute in the moment was noticeable. Things didn't always behave the way I expected, and it was hard to course-correct mid-performance.

It ended up feeling rushed rather than built. In hindsight I should have started simpler and slower, and been more patient about when to add the next element.

That approach — one layer at a time, give it space — is what I focused on more deliberately in Roulette B.
